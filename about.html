Our History

Founded in 1828 and named after Nathaniel Macon, a distinguished North Carolina statesman, Macon County is nestled in the scenic mountains of western North Carolina. The county seat, Franklin, along with the towns of Highlands and Nantahala, offer a blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage.

Geography & Demographics

Covering approximately 519.67 square miles, Macon County boasts a diverse landscape of mountains, rivers, and valleys. As of the 2020 census, the population was 37,014, with an estimated increase to 38,412 by 2023. The county is known for its outdoor recreational opportunities, including the popular Nantahala River for whitewater rafting.

Community & Culture

Macon County is home to a vibrant community that values education, public safety, and quality of life. With a range of public services, educational institutions, and cultural events, the county continues to thrive while preserving its rich heritage.

Our Mission

To provide efficient, effective, and transparent services to the citizens and visitors of Macon County, while preserving our heritage and supporting a thriving future.
